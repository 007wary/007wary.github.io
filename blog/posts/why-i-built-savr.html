Why I Built Savr — A Developer's Story

MW
Mwnswrang Wary
Developer · Wary Dev.

I never planned to build an expense tracker. Like most developers, I had a list of "cool" project ideas — games, AI tools, social apps. An expense tracker felt too boring, too ordinary. Then my bank account told me otherwise.

The Problem

I was spending money without really knowing where it was going. I tried a few popular expense tracker apps — some were too complex, some sent my data to servers I didn't control, and most were designed for Western markets with no real thought for Indian users.

"Why do I need to create an account and sync my expenses to some company's server just to track what I spent at Swiggy?"

That question stuck with me. My financial data is personal. It should live on my device, not on a startup's database somewhere.

The Build

I started Savr as a weekend project. React Native with Expo because I wanted to ship fast. SQLite for local storage because your data belongs on your device. Google Drive for backup because you already trust Google with your files.

The first version was rough — basic expense entry, a simple list, nothing fancy. But it worked and it was fast. No loading spinners waiting for server responses. No login required to see your own data.

What Savr Became

Over the next few months it grew into something I'm genuinely proud of. Auto category detection, AI-powered budget recommendations, a spending heatmap, anomaly detection for unusual expenses, 30+ currencies with INR as default, and Google Drive backup that works silently in the background.

The architecture stayed true to the original idea: local-first, privacy-first. Your expenses are stored in SQLite on your device. Supabase only knows your name and email for authentication. The backup goes to your own Google Drive folder — I literally cannot access it.

What's Next

Savr just entered closed testing on the Google Play Store. The next milestone is production release — getting it in front of real users who want a simple, private, India-focused expense tracker.

After that, a Pro tier with recurring expenses, income tracking, and multiple accounts. But the core app — tracking expenses, setting budgets, seeing reports — will always be free.

If you're reading this and want to try Savr, download it from the Play Store and let me know what you think. Every piece of feedback helps.
