5 Simple Tips to Save More Money Every Month

MW
Mwnswrang Wary
Developer · Wary Dev.

You don't need a finance degree to save money. These five tips are practical, India-specific and actually work — especially when you use an expense tracker like Savr to stay accountable.

1
Track every expense for one month
Before you can cut spending, you need to see where it's going. Use Savr for one full month without changing your habits. The reports section will show you exactly which categories are eating your money — most people are surprised by Food and Shopping.
2
Set a monthly budget for each category
Once you know your spending patterns, set realistic budgets in Savr. The AI recommendations feature suggests budgets based on your last 3 months of data — start there, then tighten by 10-15% each month.
3
Use the 48-hour rule for non-essential purchases
Before buying anything above ₹500 that isn't food, transport or bills — wait 48 hours. Most impulse purchases feel less urgent two days later. This one habit can save ₹2,000-5,000 per month for most people.
4
Audit your recurring expenses every 3 months
Subscriptions are silent budget killers — OTT platforms, app subscriptions, gym memberships you forgot about. Savr's recurring expenses feature helps you log and see all your fixed monthly costs in one place. Cancel anything you haven't used in 30 days.
5
Pay yourself first — automate savings
On salary day, immediately transfer a fixed amount to a separate savings account or SIP. Even ₹1,000/month in an index fund grows significantly over 5-10 years. Treat savings as a non-negotiable expense, not what's left over at the end of the month.

The common thread across all five tips is awareness. When you know exactly where your money is going, you naturally make better decisions. That's why tracking matters more than any specific strategy.
